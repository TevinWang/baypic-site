The Bay Area Youth Policy Innovation Challenge

About

Have some free time during the shelter-in-place? Now is the perfect time to think about ways to solve problems in your own community!

The Bay Area Youth Policy Innovation Challenge is your opportunity to make an impact on your local community on the issues you care about relating to the current COVID-19 pandemic, from healthcare affordability to workers rights.

Prompt

Even if you have never written a policy before, or you aren’t super engaged in politics, this competition is for you. We will provide all the resources you need to write a successful policy. Remember, this isn’t a formal paper, just a presentation.

PRIZES: The first place winner will receive $50 in Amazon gift cards, and the top finalists will receive up to $200 in credits from Bugsee, a leading technology company that specializes in fixing software bugs.

DEADLINE TO SIGN UP: Before Round 1 is due, July 1.


How to Sign Up

Fill out the Google Form(available on the 1st page).

After signing up, you will receive an email containing the Round 1 form where you can turn in your policy.

Details

Our Story: We are a group of high schoolers who are part of a high school politics club. We recognized that so many issues affected the youth, yet there was no true way for them to get their voice heard. Especially during this pandemic, these issues are being magnified on a larger and larger scale. That’s why we started the Bay Area Policy Innovation Challenge.

You can write your policy as a paper (2-4 pages) or as a presentation (up to 15 slides). We will also be accepting video submissions. Your work must be original. We will be checking papers and videos for plagiarism.

The competition is an online submission, due by July 1. You will be judged on
- How clearly defined the problem is
- How well you used evidence and sources to back your argument
- Highest marks will be given to papers that use unbiased, academic sources. We strongly discourage using opinion pieces, since we want you to be original with your work.
- How clearly defined your solution is
- How you addressed the opposing viewpoints

Try to be as specific as you can, but you don’t need to get too involved with the details.
